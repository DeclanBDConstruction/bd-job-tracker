Dust, Cutting & Silica — Risk Assessment

Generic template generated 02/07/2026 · Risk rating: High · Relevant legislation: COSHH 2002, Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015

Hazards Identified

  • Inhalation of dust (including respirable crystalline silica from cutting masonry, concrete, tile or brick)
  • Long-term lung damage / silicosis
  • Eye irritation
  • Fire risk from combustible dust

Who Might Be Harmed

Operatives carrying out cutting/grinding work, and others in the vicinity who may breathe in dust.

Control Measures

  • Use on-tool extraction (M-class vacuum) or water suppression when cutting, chasing, drilling or grinding.
  • Favour low-dust methods — e.g. quiet cut-off saws with water feed — over dry cutting where practical.
  • Carry out dusty work in a segregated area or outdoors where possible; screen off from other trades.
  • Damp down debris before sweeping; never dry-sweep fine dust — vacuum with an M-class vacuum instead.
  • Fit-tested RPE (FFP3 or better) to be worn for any task where dust cannot be fully controlled.
  • Keep exposure times as short as practical and rotate operatives on prolonged dusty tasks.

PPE Required

  • FFP3 (or better) respirator, fit-tested
  • Safety glasses / goggles
  • Coveralls for heavy dust work
This is a generic risk assessment. It must be reviewed and adjusted for the specific site and task conditions before anyone works to it, and re-issued if conditions change.
